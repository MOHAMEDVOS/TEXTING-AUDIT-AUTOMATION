# Funnels & Rebuttals — Sales Framework

A complete sales framework for real estate wholesaling covering lead qualification (the three funnels), information gathering (the four pillars), objection handling (the three rebuttals), SMS seller scripts, follow-up sequences, and lead labeling.

**Important context:** These are not blind contacts. Before reaching out, you already have the property address and basic info about it. Use that — referencing the address and what you know builds credibility. Never ask questions you already have the answer to. The maximum closing timeline is 6 months, but never reveal that limit to the owner — just present yourself as flexible on timing.

---

## Labels & Definitions

Labels track where a lead is in the pipeline and what stage of information gathering they're at.

**Funnel labels:**
- **WF (Wide Funnel)** — Interest in selling confirmed. Entry point. The owner raised their hand but no pillars collected yet.
- **MF (Middle Funnel)** — Motivation confirmed. The owner has shared 1–2 pillars. Conversation is real but deal isn't confirmed.
- **NF (Narrow Funnel)** — Condition + Price + Motivation + Timeline all answered. This is a deal conversation.

**Follow-up stage labels (used when a lead goes silent mid-conversation):**
- **WL (Waiting on Condition)** — Asked about condition, no response yet.
- **AP (Waiting on Asking Price)** — Asked about price, no response yet.
- **HL (Waiting on Timeline)** — Asked about closing timeline, no response yet.
- **Reason FU (Waiting on Motivation)** — Asked about reason for selling, no response yet.

**Other labels:**
- **Listed** — Property is currently listed on the market. Do not pursue.
- **Duplicate** — Lead already exists in the system. Do not pursue.
- **Bluffer** — Owner appears to be bluffing or wasting time. Don't push. If unsure, check with TL.
- **Not Interested** — All rebuttals exhausted, owner is a hard no. Stop texting.

**Push Lead criteria (when to escalate):**
- WF: Interest in selling confirmed (not listed or duplicate)
- MF: Motivation confirmed (not listed or duplicate)
- NF: Condition + Price + Motivation + Timeline all answered (not listed or duplicate)

## The Three Funnels

Funnels define how qualified a lead is — from simple interest to a near-deal.

### Wide Funnel (WF)

The owner has "raised their hand." Any signal of interest counts. The bar is intentionally low.

**What counts as a hand raise — any response that isn't a hard no:**

- Clear yes signals: "Sure, tell me more" / "Yeah I've thought about it" / "How does that work?"
- Soft or hesitant signals (these still count): "I mean… I don't know, maybe" / "Depends on the price I guess" / "Not right now but…" — anything with a "but" after it
- Curiosity questions (these absolutely count): "Are you a real estate agent?" / "How do you guys work?" — any question they ask back is a hand raise

**What does NOT count:** "Not interested" with nothing after it, "Take me off your list," or hanging up / no reply.

**How to handle:** The only goal is keeping the conversation warm and moving. No closing, no interrogation. Stay conversational and make the owner feel heard. Before pushing the lead, check if the property is a duplicate or currently listed.

**Mindset:** The wide funnel is a temperature check, not a sales moment. Sort people into "open" and "not open" — then stay in the conversation long enough for the open ones to move themselves into the middle funnel.

### Middle Funnel (MF)

The owner has shared one or two of the four pillars. The conversation is getting real, but the deal isn't confirmed.

Sharing even one pillar signals a shift from vague interest to specific disclosure — it requires trust and almost always leads to more. Requiring all four too early kills deals. The middle funnel gives owners a comfortable middle ground.

In the SMS flow, the MF stage focuses on motivation first:
> "If you don't mind me asking, could you share a bit about the reason for selling? I completely understand if it's personal."

If they give a valid motivation → Push Lead. If vague or no reason, move to the narrow funnel to collect more info.

**Mindset:** This is where most skill gets applied — guiding someone up the trust ladder, one comfortable question at a time, without them ever feeling pushed.

### Narrow Funnel (NF)

All four pillars are on the table. The owner isn't just interested — they're close to a transaction. You're no longer prospecting; you're negotiating.

**NF question sequence in SMS:**

**1. Condition:**
> "I can get an offer over to you! I just want to confirm, I'm quickly checking online and it looks like this is a [# beds] and [# baths] home. Is that correct? And have you done any repairs in the last 4 to 5 years?"

**2. Asking price (adapt based on condition answer):**

If no major updates:
> "Got it, thanks for letting me know! If you could sell your home as-is for a cash deal and we covered all of the closing costs and fees, where do you think you'd need to be on price?"

If updated:
> "Oh, that's great! Sounds like you've put a lot of work into the place. If you could sell your home as-is for a cash deal and we covered all of the closing costs and fees, where do you think you'd need to be on price?"

**Evaluate the asking price:**
- If above market → "I appreciate the reply, but that is going to be more than I can pay. If by chance you know anyone looking to sell, I pay $1,000 on all referrals I close on!" (End conversation)
- If reasonable or below market → Move to next question

**3. Motivation and closing timeline:**
> "Okay, that sounds good. Also, I'm curious, what is it that's making you consider a deal right now? And what kind of timeline would you have in mind for closing?"

If only motivation is given:
> "Got it, do you think we can do the closing within 6 months?"

If answered → Push Lead.

**What to watch for beyond the pillars:**

- **Consistency:** Do the answers line up? A high asking price + poor condition + financial urgency = tension in the story.
- **Emotional readiness:** Hedging language ("I think," "probably," "we'll see") vs. quiet certainty. True narrow funnel leads have already had the hard internal conversation.
- **Specificity:** Vague answers that technically cover all four pillars aren't the same as specific ones.

**Mindset shift:** By the narrow funnel, the dynamic has flipped. The owner is doing the talking. Your job becomes listening carefully, confirming details, and moving toward a concrete next step.

## The Four Pillars

The four pieces of information that determine whether a lead is worth pursuing seriously:

1. **Condition of the house** — What state is the property in? An owner who volunteers this is already thinking practically about the sale.
2. **Asking price** — What number does the owner have in their head? Even a ballpark signals they've been thinking seriously. This is your starting point for negotiation.
3. **Motivation for selling** — The most powerful pillar. Why they want to sell shapes everything — urgency, flexibility, emotional attachment, willingness to negotiate.
4. **Closing timeline** — How soon do they need this done? A tight timeline means they need a solution, not just an offer.

**Pillar progression through funnels:**
- Wide funnel: zero pillars — just openness
- Middle funnel: 1–2 pillars — a real conversation is happening
- Narrow funnel: all 4 pillars — this is a deal conversation

**The combination effect:** The four pillars don't just add up, they multiply each other. A motivated seller with a tight timeline, realistic price expectations, and honest condition assessment has essentially pre-qualified themselves.

## SMS Seller Script — Full Flow

### Initial Outreach (Automated — WF Entry Point)

> "Hey [first name], I'd love to chat about opportunities for your property at [address]. I'm available when you are. — Jack with LHB"

### If They Reply with Interest or Questions

Common responses: "What's this about?" / "Who is this?" / "Why are you texting me?" / "Yes" or any soft interest

Response:
> "Thanks for getting back to me. Texting you regarding your property at [address], do you have any plans to sell it soon or down the road?"

### If They Reply "No" to Initial Text

**First rebuttal:**
> "No worries, I was just wondering if you would like to sell [address]."

**Second rebuttal (if they still say no):**
> "Not a problem at all. We can start the process whenever you are ready. We're very flexible on timeline. Do you think it could be for sale within the next 6 months?"

**Final rebuttal (if still no):**
> "Understood. Do you have anything, or know someone who wants to maybe sell? I usually look for homes that need some work. I pay $1,000 for referrals I close on."

After the final rebuttal → Stop texting. Mark as Not Interested.

### Special Case — "Wrong Number"

> "I'm so sorry about that! Would you by chance know someone who may want or need to sell their home? We pay up to $1,000 for any referral you bring to us!"

### Special Case — "Call Me"

> "Would love to but, before we hop on a call, can you tell me a bit more about the house? I would hate to waste your time if it's not the right fit."

If they insist:
> "Totally understandable! When would be a good time for a quick call? I'll make sure to reach out then."

### Special Case — Bluffing

Don't push. Label as Bluffer and move on. If unsure, check with TL.

## Follow-Up Sequences (If No Response)

**Timing:** First follow-up later the same day. Second follow-up 2 days after last. Third follow-up 2 days after last.

### WL — After Asking Condition

**WL FU1:**
> "Hi, just checking in on [address]. Were there any updates, or does everything still look correct? Let me know!"

**WL FU2:**
> "[First name], is this home something you still want to sell?"

**WL FU3:**
> "Hi [first name]! It looks like this isn't something you're thinking about selling. Just to let you know, we do pay $1,000 referrals for any home you send us that we close on! Have a good day!"

### AP — After Asking Price

**AP FU1:**
> "Hey, just reaching out if you had a price in mind of what you'd want for [address]?"

**AP FU2:**
> "Hello [first name]. It's okay if not, I can get you a price. Just checking if it's something you still want to sell."

**AP FU3:**
> "Touching base [first name]. Are you still thinking to possibly sell your property at [address]?"

### HL — After Asking Timeline

**HL FU1:**
> "Hello [first name], just touching base. Was this something you wanted to sell soon or down the road?"

**HL FU2:**
> "[First name], I want to get a price over to you. Just checking in to see if you're still considering selling."

**HL FU3:**
> "Hey there, I don't want to be a bother. I'd love to purchase [address]. Are you no longer interested in selling?"

### Reason FU — After Asking Motivation

**Reason FU1:**
> "Hi, just checking on [address], do you have a particular reason you want to sell for?"

**Reason FU2:**
> "[First name], it's okay if there is not. I totally understand if it's personal, is this something you still want to sell?"

**Reason FU3:**
> "Hey! Just checking in one last time. If you're still thinking about the sale, I'd love to hear your thoughts."

## The Three Rebuttals (Call Framework)

Rebuttals prevent the conversation from ending too early and find new opportunities — even from a dead end. They are not about pressure. They run through the entire funnel flow.

### Rebuttal 1 — The Future Rebuttal

**Use when:** Owner says "not interested right now."

**Purpose:** Turn a flat "no" into a future pipeline lead by testing whether "not interested" really means "never" or just "not now."

**Script — initial pivot:**
> "I completely get that, no pressure at all. I'm just curious though — is it more of a 'never' situation, or is it something you might consider down the road? Because we're not in a rush either, we're building relationships in the area for the long term."

**If they say "maybe down the road" — this is a hand raise. Stay in the conversation and start pulling pillars:**
> "That's totally fine — and honestly, that works for us too. We're flexible on timing, so no rush at all. I'm looking at the property on [address] right now actually — I'm just curious, have you had to put much work into it recently or has it been pretty low-maintenance?"

Since you already have the address and property info, use it. Referencing the address builds credibility. Then transition into the condition pillar naturally. From here, keep going — the owner has opened the door, so gently work toward the other pillars the way you would with any wide-to-middle funnel lead.

**If they give a rough timeline like "maybe in a few months" — same approach, stay in it:**
> "That works perfectly on our end. Hey, since I've got the property pulled up — what's your sense of what it's worth right now? Just ballpark, I'm not holding you to anything."

They've already given you a timeline pillar — now pull the asking price pillar while the conversation is warm.

**If their timeline is beyond 6 months — don't promise what you can't deliver, but don't slam the door either:**
> "Got it — that makes sense. Things can shift pretty quickly though, so if anything changes on your end before then, you've got my number. And hey, if you know anyone else in the area who might be looking to sell sooner, I'd love an introduction."

This gracefully transitions into the other properties / referral rebuttal without committing to a timeline you can't honor.

**If they're clearly just being polite and want off the phone — then schedule the follow-up:**
> "Totally understand. Would it be cool if I just checked back in with you in a couple months? No pitch, no pressure — just a quick hello to see where you're at."

Only use the callback close when the owner is clearly not engaging further. If they're talking, keep the conversation alive.

**Key tone notes:**
- "Is it more of a never, or…" does a lot of work — easy out that keeps you in the picture
- When they crack the door, walk through it — transition into pillar questions naturally
- You already have the address and property info — use it
- Only default to a callback close if the owner is clearly disengaging
- Never promise a timeline beyond 6 months — if they're further out, pivot to a referral or keep it open-ended

### Rebuttal 2 — Other Properties

**Use when:** Owner says no on the current property.

**Purpose:** Open a completely different track without pushing on the same door.

**Script — pivot to other properties:**
> "Totally understand, I respect that. Hey, quick question while I have you — do you happen to own any other properties? Doesn't have to be right now, even something you've been sitting on and thinking about down the line… we're actively buying in the area and I just want to make sure I'm talking to the right people."

**If they say no to other properties too — referral pivot:**
> "No worries at all. Last thing — is there anyone in your circle, a neighbor, a family member, maybe someone who's mentioned wanting to move or downsize? I'm not looking to bother anyone, I'd just reach out real quick and introduce myself."

**Key tone notes:**
- Keep it light and unhurried
- "While I have you" is a natural transition phrase
- End with an easy yes/no question, not an open-ended one

### Rebuttal 3 — Wrong Number

**Use when:** The person says you have the wrong number.

**Purpose:** Turn a dead end into a brief pitch — you have maybe 10 seconds.

**Script — standard version:**
> "Oh, my apologies for that! Hey, before I let you go — I know this is a bit random, but I'm actually in the business of buying properties in the area. If you or anyone you know ever has a property they're thinking about selling, I'd love to be a resource. No pressure at all — just wanted to put it out there while I had you."

**Shorter version — if they sound impatient:**
> "Sorry about that! Real quick — we buy properties in the area, so if you ever think about selling or know someone who might be, feel free to keep my number. Hope you have a great day."

**If they surprisingly engage — "actually I might have something":**
> "Oh really? That's great timing then — happy accident! Tell me a little about the property, what are you working with?"

**Key tone notes:**
- Lead with a genuine apology — it earns you the next two sentences
- The whole pitch needs to fit in one breath
- Never ask for details unless they open the door first — then switch into funnel mode

## Generating Scripts and Coaching

When the user asks for scripts, roleplay, or coaching advice:

1. **Identify the funnel stage and rebuttal context** — what scenario are they dealing with?
2. **Match the channel** — SMS scripts are shorter and more direct. Call scripts are warmer and more conversational.
3. **Respect the framework's philosophy** — rebuttals are about creating openings, not applying pressure. The goal is always to keep the conversation alive.
4. **Use the pillar progression** — guide naturally from wide to narrow without making the owner feel interrogated.
5. **Adapt to the specific scenario** — the scripts above are templates. Customize based on the owner's responses, property type, and situation the user describes.
6. **Use correct labels** — when classifying leads or discussing pipeline stages, use the label codes (WF, MF, NF, WL, AP, HL, etc.) consistently.

When roleplaying as a prospect, vary your responses realistically — some owners are warm, some are curt, some are suspicious. This helps the user practice adapting in real time.
