How the Audit Works

A plain-English guide to what gets checked, how scores are calculated,
and how the system decides if something is a red flag.

📋

What is an audit?

Every text conversation gets read by an AI that acts like a senior manager reviewing the agent's work.

🎯

What does it check?

Did the agent follow the script? Was the tone professional? Did they miss any red flags?

⚡

How fast?

Each conversation is scored in about 5-10 seconds. 10 conversations takes under 2 minutes.

🤖

Who decides?

The AI (Groq LLM) reads every message and applies your company's exact rules — not generic rules.

Section 2 — The Scores

The 4 Scores (Each Out of 100)

Every conversation gets 4 separate scores. Think of them as 4 questions a manager would ask after reading the conversation.

✅

1. Compliance (Adherence)

Question: Did the agent respect opt-outs?

If someone said "stop texting me" or "remove me" — did the agent stop? This score drops to zero if the agent kept texting after an explicit stop request.

😊

2. Attitude (Sentiment)

Question: Was the agent's tone good?

Was the agent warm, empathetic, and conversational? Or cold, pushy, and robotic? Note: If the LEAD was rude, the agent's score does NOT drop — only the agent's tone is judged.

💼

3. Professionalism

Question: Did the agent act professionally?

Only big mistakes count: wrong name used, incoherent sentences, mixed up property details. Minor typos and casual language are NOT penalized.

📜

4. Script Adherence

Question: Did the agent follow the playbook?

Did they gather the right information? Use the rebuttals? Offer the $1,000 referral at the right moment? This measures whether the agent followed the correct sales process.

Section 3 — The Sales Funnel

How the AI Reads the Conversation

The AI first classifies what TYPE of conversation it is, then applies the right rules for that type.

Step 1 — What type is this?

A

Normal Seller

Lead owns the property and may sell.

B

Wrong Number

Person has nothing to do with the property.

C

Wrong Property

Lead owns a DIFFERENT property than listed.

D

Referral

Lead offers someone else who wants to sell.

E

Realtor / Investor

Lead is a real estate professional.

Step 2 — How far did the sale progress?

For normal sellers, the AI checks how deep into the "funnel" the agent got:

Wide Funnel (WF)
Just making contact — any positive signal counts
Labels: Potential, New Lead, Stopped Responding
Middle Funnel (MF)
1-2 pieces of key info gathered from the lead
Labels: Warm, WL drip, AP drip
Narrow Funnel (NF)
All 4 pillars gathered — ready to book a call
Labels: Hot, Lead, Lead Pushed
Section 4 — The 4 Pillars

The 4 Pillars: What the Agent Needs to Find Out

For a serious seller conversation, the agent needs to collect 4 key pieces of information — called "pillars." The AI checks whether the agent actually got these from the lead, in their own words.

🏠

Pillar 1 — Condition

What state is the property in? Any repairs needed? The lead must describe it themselves — the agent can't assume.

Example: "Needs a new roof and the kitchen is outdated"

💰

Pillar 2 — Asking Price

What price would the lead want? They must give a specific number. "How much can you offer?" does NOT count — that is the lead's question, not their answer.

Example: "I'd need at least $250,000"

❓

Pillar 3 — Motivation

WHY are they considering selling? The lead must tell the agent their reason — not just say "yes" or nod along.

Example: "We're downsizing, kids moved out"

📅

Pillar 4 — Timeline

WHEN would they want to sell? Again, the lead must state a timeframe. The agent should NEVER reveal the 6-month buying window.

Example: "Within the next 3 months"

⚠️ Golden Rule

A pillar is only "gathered" if the lead said it in their own words. If the agent asked but the lead didn't answer — it does NOT count as gathered. The AI will only mark a pillar as collected if it can quote the lead's exact response.

Section 5 — The Rebuttals

When a Lead Says "No" — The 3 Rebuttals

A lead saying "No" or "Not interested" is NOT a reason to stop. The correct playbook says the agent must try 3 rebuttals before giving up. The AI checks whether they did this.

1

Future Rebuttal — "Maybe later?"

"Is it more of a never, or something you might consider down the road?"
Goal: find out if they might sell in the future, even if not now.

↓ If still no
2

Other Properties — "Do you own anything else?"

"Do you happen to own any other properties you'd consider selling?"
Goal: pivot to any other property they might own.

↓ If still no
3

Referral Close — "$1,000 for a referral"

"Know someone who wants to sell? I pay $1,000 for referrals I close on."
Goal: exit gracefully and get a potential new lead.

↓
✕

After all 3 rebuttals — STOP

Label as "Not Interested." Do not send more messages. Any message after this = red flag.

✅ Important: "No" is NOT a stop signal

"No", "Not interested", "NO!!!" — these are all soft rejections. The Future Rebuttal is the REQUIRED response. The AI will NEVER flag an agent for continuing after a soft no.

Section 6 — Red Flags

What Counts as a Red Flag

A red flag is only raised when an agent made a clear, specific mistake that a manager would need to coach them on. Borderline situations do NOT get a flag — the AI defaults to "no flag" when unsure.

🚩 These ARE red flags

  • 🚫

    Texting after explicit opt-out

    Lead said "stop texting", "remove me", "unsubscribe", "leave me alone" — and agent kept going.

  • 😡

    Aggressive or deceptive language

    Agent used threatening, dishonest, or manipulative wording.

  • 💸

    Giving a firm dollar offer

    Agent said "I'll pay you $200,000" — agents are NOT allowed to make offers over text.

  • 🏳️

    Gave up after first "no"

    Lead said "not interested" and agent immediately stopped with zero rebuttals attempted.

  • 📞

    Booked a call without qualifying first

    Agent jumped straight to "let's hop on a call!" before gathering any pillars.

  • 🤦

    Incoherent or embarrassing message

    Sent a message that made no sense or used the wrong name for the lead.

✅ These are NOT red flags

  • 💬

    Sending multiple follow-up texts

    This is the job. Wide funnel drip sequences require multiple messages without a response.

  • 🔄

    Continuing after "No" or "Not interested"

    Soft rejections require the rebuttal sequence. The agent MUST continue — stopping early is the mistake.

  • ✏️

    Minor typos or casual tone

    Informal language, abbreviations, relaxed punctuation — all fine. Only major errors count.

  • ❓

    "How did you get my number?"

    This is a neutral question. Agent answers it and continues — never a flag.

  • 💵

    Mentioning the $1,000 referral

    This is a required scripted exit line. The AI scores it positively, never flags it.

  • 😤

    Rude lead, professional agent

    If the lead was hostile but the agent stayed professional, the score stays high — the lead's behavior is never judged.

Section 7 — Real Examples

Red Flag vs. No Flag — Side by Side

Example A — RED FLAG

Agent (Jack)
Hi Franklin, have you thought about selling your property at 2142 Wilburn Ave?
Franklin
I am not selling my house. Please stop contacting me.
Agent (Jack)
Hi Franklin, I wanted to see if you've considered selling your property at 2142 Wilburn Ave — let me know!
Agent (Jack)

🚩 RED FLAG — Compliance score drops

"Please stop contacting me" is an explicit opt-out. Agent sent 2 more messages after that. This is the only real line that can never be crossed.

Karl
No 👎
Agent (Jack)
Not a problem at all. Do you think it could be for sale within the next 6 months?
Karl
No
Agent (Jack)
Understood. Know someone who wants to sell? I pay $1,000 for referrals I close on.

✅ NO FLAG — Perfect script execution

"No" is a soft rejection. Agent used Future Rebuttal, then Referral Close. Textbook correct. High script adherence score.

Section 8 — The ML Pre-Filter

How the ML Saves Time & Money

Running every conversation through Groq AI costs money and quota. The ML pre-filter is a "shortcut checker" that handles obvious cases for free.

Tier 1 — Phrase Check Free · <1ms

Scans for specific words: "stop texting", "remove me", "wrong number." If found → flags immediately without reading the whole conversation. If clean → passes to Tier 2.

Tier 2 — Similarity Check Free · ~5ms

Compares this conversation to 340 past conversations. If it looks very similar to conversations that were "clean" before → copy their score. Currently at 340 stored conversations — needs 2,000 to become highly effective.

Tier 3 — Pattern Predictor Free · ~10ms

A trained model that predicts the 4 scores based on patterns it learned from past Groq decisions. If confident it's clean → skip Groq. If any doubt → send to Groq.

Tier 4 — Groq AI (Full Audit) Costs quota · ~8 seconds

Full audit by the AI. Reads every message, applies all rules, produces 4 scores + red flags + summary. Only conversations that passed through all 3 filters reach here.
