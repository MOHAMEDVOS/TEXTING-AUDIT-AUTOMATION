Lead Stage Labels
How qualified is the lead right now?
New Lead
Stage 0
First time you reached out. The lead hasn't said anything meaningful yet — or hasn't replied at all.
Example
Agent → "Hey John, I'd love to chat about your property at 123 Main St. — Jack"
Lead → (no reply)
New Lead
Potential
Stage 1 · WF
The lead showed any sign of interest — even soft or hesitant. They didn't say a hard no. Anything with a "but" after it still counts.
Example
Lead → "Depends on the price I guess…"
Lead → "How does that work?"
Lead → "Maybe, not sure yet"
Potential
Warm
Stage 2 · MF
The lead is genuinely engaged and has shared 1–2 real pieces of information — condition, price range, reason for selling, or timeline.
Example
Lead → "Yeah the house needs work. Been thinking about selling."
Agent knows: condition + motivation
Warm
Hot
Stage 3 · NF
Seriously interested. All 4 key questions are on the table — condition, price, reason, and timeline. This is a real deal conversation.
Example
Lead → "Roof needs work, want $180k, going through divorce, need to close in 3 months."
All 4 pillars: condition price motivation timeline
Hot
Lead
Fully Qualified
Fully qualified with specific answers to all 4 pillars. You're no longer prospecting — you're negotiating. Ready to be pushed to a closer.
Example
Lead → "It's a 3/2, needs updates. I'd want around $175k cash. Going through a divorce, trying to close in 60 days."
Lead
Investor
Not a Seller
The person you're talking to is a buyer or investor — not the property owner. They can't sell you anything.
Example
Lead → "Oh I don't own this, I just buy and flip properties in the area."
Investor
02
Follow-Up Stage Labels
What follow-up was sent, and what are you waiting on?
FU1
Same Day
First follow-up. Sent the same day the lead went quiet after a question.
Example
Agent asked about condition at 10am. No reply by evening.
Agent → "Hi, just checking in on 123 Main St. Were there any updates?"
FU1
FU2
+2 Days
Second follow-up. Lead still hasn't replied after FU1. Wait 2 days, then send.
Example
FU1 sent Monday. No response.
Wednesday → "John, is this home something you still want to sell?"
FU2
FU3
+2 Days · Final
Third and final follow-up. Sent 2 days after FU2. After this — stop texting this lead.
Example
FU2 sent Wednesday. No response.
Friday → "Hi John! Looks like this isn't top of mind. We pay $1,000 referrals if you know anyone. Have a great day!"
FU3 — then stop
WL drip
Waiting on Condition
You asked about the condition of the property. The lead hasn't answered yet. You're in the follow-up sequence waiting on that answer.
Example
Agent → "Have you done any repairs in the last 4–5 years?"
Lead → (no reply)
WL drip
AP drip
Waiting on Price
You asked what price they're looking for. The lead hasn't answered yet.
Example
Agent → "If you could sell as-is for cash, where would you need to be on price?"
Lead → (no reply)
AP drip
HL drip
Waiting on Timeline
You asked about their closing timeline. The lead hasn't answered yet.
Example
Agent → "What kind of timeline would you have in mind for closing?"
Lead → (no reply)
HL drip
Reason FU
Waiting on Motivation
You asked why they want to sell. The lead hasn't answered yet.
Example
Agent → "Could you share a bit about the reason for selling? Totally understand if it's personal."
Lead → (no reply)
Reason FU
Waiting to be pushed
Ready to Escalate
The lead is fully qualified — all 4 pillars are confirmed. The agent is done. This needs to go to the closer now.
Example
Lead gave: condition price motivation timeline
Agent hasn't handed it off yet.
Waiting to be pushed
Pushed to client
Handed Off
Done. The lead has been handed off to the buyer or closer. The agent's job on this one is finished.
Example
Agent sent the full deal summary to the buyer team.
Pushed to client
03
Outcome Labels
What was the final result of this conversation?
Deal closed
Closed
The deal is done. Transaction completed successfully.
Example
Property at 123 Main St. signed, funded, and closed.
Deal closed
Sold
Property Sold
The property has been sold — confirmed by the lead.
Example
Lead → "We already sold the house last month actually."
Sold
04
Rejection & Disqualification Labels
Why is this lead dead, paused, or disqualified?
Not Interested
Soft No · Stop
The lead said no — and you used all 3 rebuttals. They didn't demand removal, they just don't want to sell. Stop texting after this.
Example
Agent used all 3 rebuttals. Lead kept saying no. Never said "remove me."
Not Interested — stop texting
Verified
Confirmed Not Interested
Not interested confirmed across at least 2 separate contacts. Not just a one-time brush-off — this one is double-confirmed.
Example
First contact → "Not interested."
Follow-up 2 days later → "Still no, not looking to sell."
Verified
Note: Do NOT use after just one "no"
Maybe Later
Future Pipeline
Not ready to sell now — but left the door open. Keep them in the pipeline and check back.
Example
Lead → "Not right now, maybe in about 6 months."
Agent → "Totally fine, I'll check back in with you."
Maybe Later
Stopped Responding
Ghosted
The lead was engaged and talking — then just disappeared. No hard no, no opt-out, just silence after the follow-up sequence.
Example
Lead replied twice, was asking questions — then never responded again after the price question. FU1, FU2, FU3 sent. No response.
Stopped Responding
Bluffer
Not Serious
The lead is not being serious. Usually they throw out a ridiculous price to make you go away — but they never said stop. Don't label as DO Not Call unless they actually used opt-out language.
Example
Lead → "$2 million. That's what I want." (house worth ~$150k)
Bluffer + Abv MV
Note: Do NOT label Warm, Hot, or DO Not Call
Abv MV
Above Market Value
The lead's price expectation is way too high — not realistic for a cash/as-is deal. Often paired with Bluffer.
Example
Agent → "Where would you need to be on price?"
Lead → "I want $500k." (property worth ~$200k)
Abv MV
DO Not Call
Hard Stop
The lead explicitly told you to stop — used clear language like "stop texting", "remove me", or "don't contact me again." This is a compliance issue. Stop immediately — no more messages ever.
Example
Lead → "Stop texting me. Remove me from your list."
Lead → "Do not contact me again."
DO Not Call — zero contact from now on
A plain "No" or "Not interested" is NOT enough for this label
Disqualified
Can't Sell
The lead doesn't qualify — they're not the owner, don't have authority to sell, or the property simply doesn't fit.
Example
Lead → "Oh this isn't my house, I'm just renting it."
Disqualified
Listed
On the Market
The property is already listed with a real estate agent. Do not pursue — it's not a direct seller situation.
Example
Lead → "It's already listed with a realtor."
Listed — do not pursue
Duplicate
Already Exists
This contact or property already exists somewhere in the system under another record.
Example
Agent notices this lead was already worked by a team member last month under a different number.
Duplicate — do not pursue
Wrong Number
Wrong Person
You reached the wrong person entirely — they don't own that property and have no connection to it.
Example
Lead → "I think you have the wrong number, I don't own any property."
Wrong Number
TIP
Multiple labels are normal — and often required.
For example: Warm + AP drip means the lead shared some info but you're now waiting on their price. Or FU2 + WL drip means you're on the second follow-up waiting for their condition answer. Always use as many labels as apply to accurately reflect where the lead is.
